Are the Sitges authorities walking into disaster?

Basic Info

As part of my A-Level Geography course I am required to create a 4000 word document In July 2017 we visited the town of Sitges, located within Catalonia, east of Barcelona. While we were there we undertook fieldwork on the beaches. Sitges, and the Catalonian coastline overall, is struggling significantly with coastal erosion. Defences have been set up over the years and our task was aimed at concluding how successful they have been.

Fieldwork

To assist me with my coursework I used numerous fieldwork techniques:

First of all, at a location towards the west of the town, we measured both the size (through the pebble's long axis) and orientation of the pebbles. this technique was used to find the direction of longshore drift. Theoretically pebble size should decrease with longshore drift, and orientation should be aligned in the same direction.

Secondly, at the beaches directly within the town, we performed a number of beach assessments. These included measuring:

-beach profile

-beach width

-beach depth

-promenade length

All of these were used to help get a clear picture of which defences are working and which aren't

Analysis

My analysis of the question 'Are the Sitges authorities sleep walking into disaster?' drove me to the conclusion that, while there are many things the authorities could implement to solve the issues, if the situation continues to develop in its current state the coastline will fall into turmoil

The vast majority of defences in place have not worked, with the L-Head breakwaters at the far end of the beach resulting in erosion of 100% of the beach material. The beach nourishment scheme in place has also not helped, despite costing millions of euros.
